Project Overview

The Global Terrorism Index (GTI) provides critical data on terrorism trends worldwide. Our project enhances the visualization of this data to improve interpretability while preserving data integrity. Through better visualization techniques, we enable clearer understanding of terrorism patterns across countries and time periods.

Original Visualization Challenges

  • ❌ Difficult to distinguish year-on-year trends
  • ❌ Poor use of color gradients
  • ❌ Limited contextual information
  • ✅ Included core metrics (Score, Rank, etc.)

Our Improvements

  • ✅ Clear temporal trends visualization (2012-2022)
  • ✅ Discrete color coding by country
  • ✅ Geographic clustering by continent
  • ✅ Correlation analysis of GTI scores and fatalities

Key Insights

Temporal Patterns

Afghanistan, Iraq, and Syria consistently ranked among the highest GTI scores over the decade, with visible trends showing shifts in terrorism hotspots.

Geographic Clustering

Clear continental clustering of terrorism activity, with significant hotspots in the Middle East and parts of Africa.

Score vs. Impact

While GTI scores generally correlate with fatalities, some countries have high scores despite fewer deaths, suggesting the index incorporates other risk factors.

Data-Driven Insights

Our visualization reveals patterns that were obscured in the original presentation, enabling better-informed security and policy decisions.
